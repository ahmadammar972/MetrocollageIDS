Module 3 · Scales

Measurement Scales

Differentiating how quantitative variables relate to the mathematical concept of "Zero".

Interval Scale

Order matters, and distances are exact. However, there is no true zero representing a total absence.

Example: FICO Credit Score
A score of 300 doesn't mean "zero creditworthiness", and you cannot mathematically say a score of 600 is "twice as good" as 300.

Ratio Scale

Order matters, distances are exact, AND there is a clear, absolute zero point.

Example: Annual Recurring Revenue
$0 ARR means a literal absence of revenue. A client generating $100k ARR is exactly twice as valuable as a $50k ARR client.

Module 3 · Central Tendency

Central Tendency: Mean vs Median

Mean: The mathematical average.

Median: The exact middle record when sorted sequentially.

The "Whale" Problem: Medians are highly resilient to extreme outliers. This is why national real estate prices and household incomes are always reported in medians—one billionaire skews the mean, making it a terrible representation of reality.
